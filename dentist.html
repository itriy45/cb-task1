Welcome, Doctor. You've just clocked in for your first rotation at Code Black Memorial Hospital.

Urgent

🚑 Trauma Bay 1

Scenario

A 24-year-old cyclist was just brought in after a heavy crash. They are holding their shoulder in extreme pain. You review the incoming X-ray and spot a severe fracture in their collarbone.

How do you formally document the broken "collarbone" in the medical chart?

Incoming Patient

What exactly is the patient struggling with right now?

💊 Pharmacy Call

Scenario

You stabilize the patient, but notice extreme swelling and inflammation in their airways. You need to write a rapid prescription to bring the swelling down.

Which class of medication is typically prescribed to rapidly reduce inflammation?

📟 Surgical Consult

Pager Message

"Dr., we need you to prep the patient in Room 402. General surgery is taking them up for an emergent cholecystectomy in 10 minutes."

You explain the procedure to the family. What organ is being removed?

📋 Charting

Scenario

Because the patient in Room 402 is going into surgery, they cannot have any food or water to avoid aspiration risks. You pick up their medical chart.

Which standard abbreviation do you write on their chart to ensure the nurses enforce this rule?

📈 Diagnostics

Scenario

A 60-year-old woman walks into the ER clutching her chest and sweating profusely. You immediately order a STAT ECG (Electrocardiogram).

What vital organ's electrical activity is this machine primarily used to evaluate?

🩺 Routine Check

Scenario

While reviewing the ECG patient's past medical history, you notice she has been struggling with chronic hypertension for over a decade without medication.

What is the more common, everyday term for hypertension?

🥼 Referrals

Scenario

Your next patient complains of severe, unexplained abdominal pain and blood in their stool. You decide this is beyond the ER's scope and requires a specialist who deals with the digestive system.

Which specialist do you page for a consult?

⚡ Blackout!

Emergency

The hospital generators stutter! All digital bedside monitors temporarily shut off. You grab an old-school, manual sphygmomanometer from the wall cabinet.

You wrap the cuff around the patient's arm. What vital sign are you measuring?

🪡 The Kitchen Accident

Scenario

Power is back. A local chef is rushed in with a deep, clean slice across their palm from a misplaced cleaver. You clean the wound thoroughly.

"Don't worry," you tell the chef. "I'm going to _____ the wound to prevent infection and promote healing."

💀 The Bar Fight

Scenario

A college student is dropped off by police. He got into a fight downtown and took a massive punch to the face. The notes say "suspected fracture to the mandible."

Where exactly are you going to examine the patient for this fracture?

🚦 Pileup

Mass Casualty Alert

A ten-car pileup just happened on the icy highway. Thirty patients are flooding into the lobby simultaneously. The Head Nurse takes the clipboard and yells, "I'm running triage at the doors!"

What is her primary, critical job right now?

❤️‍🔥 Monitor Alarms

Scenario

You are checking on a trauma victim when their bedside monitor suddenly turns red and starts beeping aggressively. The screen flashes: Tachycardia.

Based on medical terminology roots, what does the prefix "tachy-" tell you is happening to their heart?

Scenario

It's 4:00 AM. You are doing rounds in the ICU. A patient who came in for a clean, routine knee surgery three days ago suddenly spikes a 103°F fever. You deduce they acquired this infection during their hospital stay.

What is the technical, medical term for a hospital-acquired infection?
